What is Radicals?

A radical is a mathematical symbol (√) used to represent the root of a number. It consists of a radicand, which is the number or expression under the radical sign, and a radical sign (√). Radicals are used to perform operations such as square roots, cube roots, and higher-order roots.

Radicals have various properties, including:

  • The index, which indicates the root's degree (e.g., square root has an index of 2).
  • The radicand, which is the number or expression inside the radical symbol.
  • The principal root, which is the positive root when dealing with even roots (e.g., √4 = 2).

Understanding radicals is essential in mathematics as they are used in various operations, such as simplifying expressions, solving equations, and working with geometric shapes. By grasping the concept of radicals and their properties, learners can develop a solid foundation in advanced mathematical topics.

Real-life Scenarion where Radicals are important

Example 1:

Scenario: A home improvement project requires calculating the length of a diagonal brace for a wooden frame.

Description: A carpenter is constructing a rectangular wooden frame for a window. The width of the frame is 3 feet, and the height is 4 feet. To provide stability, a diagonal brace needs to be installed from one corner of the frame to the opposite corner. The length of the diagonal brace can be determined using the Pythagorean theorem, which involves radicals.

Question: What is the length of the diagonal brace?

Solution: Let's use the Pythagorean theorem, which states that in a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Given:

Length of one side: 3 feet

Length of the other side: 2 feet

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can find the length of the diagonal brace:

Diagonal brace = √(3^2 + 2^2)

= √(9 + 4)

= √13 feet

Example 2:

Scenario: Carpeting a Room

Description: You are planning to carpet a rectangular room in your house. The length of the room is represented by the expression 2√5 meters, and the width of the room is represented by the expression √8 meters. You want to determine the simplified form of the total area of the room without converting it into decimals.

Question: What is the simplified form of the total area of the room, given the length of 2√5 meters and the width of √8 meters, without converting it into decimals?

Solution:

To find the total area of the room, we need to multiply the length by the width. Let's simplify the given expressions:

Length = 2√5 meters

Width = √8 meters

Now, let's calculate the total area:

Total Area = Length * Width

= (2√5 meters) * (√8 meters)

= 2√5 * √8 meters

= 2 * √5 * √8 meters

= 2 * √(5 * 8) meters = 2 * √40 meters
